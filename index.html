About

Aviation runs in the family — my dad and brother both fly for the airlines, and I grew up around briefings, checklists, and hangar talk long before I ever took the controls myself. That background shaped how I teach: thorough, patient, and focused on building pilots who are truly ready, not just checkride-ready.

I'm 20 years old and hold my CFI and CFI-I certificates with a clean record — every checkride of mine passed on the first attempt. I bring that same preparation to every lesson, whether we're working toward a Sport or Private certificate, an instrument rating, or just knocking out a Flight Review.

I'm genuinely excited to fly with you. Training is conducted using my partnered fleet, so you don't need to own an aircraft to get started.

Training Programs

Sport Pilot

A streamlined path to flying light-sport aircraft, with as few as 20 hours of training and no FAA medical exam required.

Private

Earn your Private Pilot Certificate with structured lessons covering cross-country flying, solo requirements, and checkride preparation.

Instrument

Build the skills and confidence to fly safely in the clouds, including approach procedures and cross-country instrument experience.

Commercial

Advance toward a Commercial Pilot Certificate with focused training in complex aircraft and long cross-country flights.

Flight Review

Stay current with a Flight Review tailored to the type of flying you do, combining ground and flight instruction.

IPC

Regain instrument currency with an Instrument Proficiency Check covering approaches, holds, and unusual attitude recovery.

Fleet

Van's RV-12iS

Training is conducted in a Van's RV-12iS, a two-seat, low-wing Light Sport Aircraft powered by a fuel-injected Rotax 912 iS engine. Modern glass avionics and responsive handling make it a great platform for both Sport and Private Pilot training.

  • EngineRotax 912 iS (fuel-injected)
  • Seats2, side-by-side
  • Wingspan26 ft 9 in
